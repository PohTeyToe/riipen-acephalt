# Acephalt Demo Product Context

register: product

## Product Purpose

This demo is a credibility-first proof-of-work artifact for Acephalt. It is not trying to pitch a whole platform. It exists to show that Abdallah understood a real diligence trust-boundary problem, scoped it narrowly, and turned that judgment into something that feels plausibly buildable.

The artifact should make a skeptical operator think:

- this person understood the workflow
- this person knows what to cut
- this could become a real internal prototype

## Users

- Primary viewer: Nicolas Frendo or another Acephalt decision-maker with finance and product instincts
- Secondary viewer: any Riipen admin, startup operator, or engineer evaluating whether the work is unusually thoughtful

## Desired Reaction

The first reaction should not be "nice student mock." It should be:

"They picked the right narrow slice, and they made the trust model legible."

The ideal follow-up is a conversation about the tradeoffs in the access model, audit posture, or policy enforcement boundary.

## Strategic Principle

This prototype makes one specific bet about Acephalt: if the product is serious about becoming part of the operating system for investment decisions, it eventually has to own more than workflow. It has to own which role can see which fact, why that decision was made, and what trace gets left behind when sensitive material is opened. Project Cedar tests that bet at the smallest believable surface: per-field policy outcomes inside a shared diligence room.

## What Must Be Proved

- The same diligence room looks meaningfully different across roles
- Field-level policy is the core idea, not a UI trick
- Audit and access posture are first-class concerns
- The builder can think in terms of enforcement, provenance, and reviewer workflow

## Anti-References

Do not turn this into:

- a generic AI dashboard
- a data room redesign for everything
- a flashy motion-heavy landing page
- fake enterprise complexity
- vague "copilot" product language

## Voice

Use direct, controlled language. No startup hype. No inflated claims. No generic "revolutionizing diligence" copy.
